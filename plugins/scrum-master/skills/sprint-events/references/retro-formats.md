# Retrospective Formats

Complete templates with step-by-step facilitation instructions for each retrospective format. Choose the format that best fits the team's current situation.

---

## Start / Stop / Continue

### Best For
General-purpose, quick retrospectives. Good default for new teams.

### Time Required
45-60 minutes.

### Materials
Three columns on a board (physical or digital): Start, Stop, Continue.

### Facilitation Steps

1. **Set the stage (5 min):** Review previous Sprint's action items. Check completion status.
2. **Generate data (10 min):** Each person writes items on sticky notes for each column:
   - **Start:** Things the team is not doing but should begin.
   - **Stop:** Things the team is doing that are not helping.
   - **Continue:** Things that are working well and should persist.
3. **Share and group (10 min):** Each person reads their notes. Facilitator groups similar items.
4. **Vote (5 min):** Each person gets 3 dots to vote on the most important items across all columns.
5. **Discuss (15-20 min):** Discuss the top 3-5 voted items. For each, identify root cause and potential action.
6. **Decide on actions (5-10 min):** Select 1-3 action items with owners and target Sprints.
7. **Close (5 min):** Summarize actions. End with something positive.

## 4Ls (Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For)

### Best For
Balanced emotional and analytical reflection. Good after eventful Sprints.

### Time Required
60-75 minutes.

### Materials
Four columns: Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For.

### Facilitation Steps

1. **Set the stage (5 min):** Review previous action items. State purpose: "We are reflecting on the Sprint across four dimensions."
2. **Generate data (10 min):** Silent writing on sticky notes for each column:
   - **Liked:** What went well? What brought satisfaction?
   - **Learned:** What new knowledge or insight was gained?
   - **Lacked:** What was missing? What caused frustration?
   - **Longed For:** What do we wish we had? What would make things better?
3. **Share and cluster (15 min):** Round-robin sharing. Facilitator groups related items.
4. **Dot vote (5 min):** 3 dots per person on the items they want to discuss or act on.
5. **Deep dive (20-30 min):** Discuss top items. Focus especially on "Lacked" and "Longed For" for improvement potential.
6. **Action items (5-10 min):** Convert insights into 1-3 concrete actions.
7. **Close (5 min):** Highlight key "Liked" and "Learned" items to end positively.

## Mad / Sad / Glad

### Best For
Emotional check-in after difficult or intense Sprints. Surfaces morale issues.

### Time Required
45-60 minutes.

### Materials
Three columns: Mad, Sad, Glad.

### Facilitation Steps

1. **Set the stage (5 min):** Acknowledge the Sprint. "This retro is about how the Sprint made us feel."
2. **Generate data (10 min):** Silent writing:
   - **Mad:** What frustrated or angered you?
   - **Sad:** What disappointed you or caused concern?
   - **Glad:** What made you happy or grateful?
3. **Share (15 min):** Each person shares their notes. No interruptions during sharing. Facilitator acknowledges each contribution.
4. **Group and vote (5 min):** Cluster related items. Dot vote (3 per person).
5. **Discuss (15-20 min):** Start with "Mad" items to address frustrations. Move to "Sad," then celebrate "Glad."
6. **Action items (5-10 min):** Focus actions on resolving "Mad" and "Sad" root causes.
7. **Close (5 min):** End with "Glad" highlights.

**Facilitation note:** This format surfaces strong emotions. Ensure psychological safety. Ground rule: "We assume everyone did their best given the circumstances."

## Sailboat

### Best For
Forward-looking planning. Good when the team feels stuck or needs direction.

### Time Required
60-75 minutes.

### Materials
Drawing of a sailboat with four elements:
- **Wind (sails):** Forces propelling us forward
- **Anchor:** Forces holding us back
- **Rocks:** Risks ahead
- **Island:** Our destination / goal

### Facilitation Steps

1. **Set the stage (5 min):** Draw the sailboat on a whiteboard. Explain each element.
2. **Define the Island (5 min):** Collaboratively state where the team wants to be. This can be the Sprint Goal, a quarterly objective, or a team aspiration.
3. **Generate data (10 min):** Silent writing for each element:
   - What is our wind? (What helps us move forward?)
   - What is our anchor? (What slows us down?)
   - What are our rocks? (What risks lie ahead?)
4. **Share and place (15 min):** Each person places their items on the diagram and explains briefly.
5. **Discuss (15-20 min):** Focus on anchors and rocks. How can we cut anchors? How can we navigate around rocks?
6. **Action items (5-10 min):** Prioritize 1-3 actions targeting the biggest anchors or most dangerous rocks.
7. **Close (5 min):** Reinforce the wind items and the team's destination.

## Timeline

### Best For
Incident analysis, event-heavy Sprints, understanding how things unfolded.

### Time Required
60-90 minutes.

### Materials
Horizontal timeline drawn across a board spanning the Sprint duration.

### Facilitation Steps

1. **Set the stage (5 min):** Draw the Sprint timeline with key dates marked (Sprint start, end, any known milestones).
2. **Generate data (15 min):** Each person writes events, decisions, and feelings on sticky notes and places them on the timeline where they occurred.
3. **Walk-through (15 min):** Facilitator walks the team through the timeline chronologically. At each cluster, ask: "What was happening here? How did it feel?"
4. **Identify patterns (10 min):** Look for:
   - Peaks and valleys in team energy/morale
   - Cause-effect chains
   - Recurring themes
5. **Deep dive (15-20 min):** Select 2-3 critical moments for deeper analysis. Use "5 Whys" to find root causes.
6. **Action items (5-10 min):** What would we do differently next time?
7. **Close (5 min):** Summarize key learnings.

## Starfish

### Best For
Nuanced feedback when Start/Stop/Continue feels too binary.

### Time Required
60-75 minutes.

### Materials
Five columns: More Of, Less Of, Keep Doing, Start Doing, Stop Doing.

### Facilitation Steps

1. **Set the stage (5 min):** Explain the five dimensions. "This gives us more nuance than just start/stop."
2. **Generate data (10 min):** Silent writing for each column:
   - **More Of:** What should we amplify?
   - **Less Of:** What should we dial back (but not eliminate)?
   - **Keep Doing:** What is working perfectly?
   - **Start Doing:** What new practices should we introduce?
   - **Stop Doing:** What should we eliminate entirely?
3. **Share and cluster (15 min):** Round-robin sharing, group related items.
4. **Vote (5 min):** 3-5 dots per person across all columns.
5. **Discuss (15-20 min):** Focus on highest-voted items. "More Of" and "Less Of" often yield subtle but impactful improvements.
6. **Action items (5-10 min):** Select 1-3 actions. Ensure at least one is from "More Of" (amplifying what works is often easier than fixing what is broken).
7. **Close (5 min):** Celebrate "Keep Doing" items.

## Format Selection Guide

| Situation | Recommended Format |
|-----------|--------------------|
| New team, first retrospective | Start/Stop/Continue |
| After a difficult Sprint | Mad/Sad/Glad |
| Team feels stuck | Sailboat |
| Incident or production issue | Timeline |
| Routine Sprint, need fresh perspective | 4Ls or Starfish |
| Team has been using same format too long | Switch to any format not used in last 3 Sprints |

### Rotation Strategy

Rotate formats every 2-3 Sprints. A sample 6-Sprint rotation:

1. Start/Stop/Continue (baseline)
2. 4Ls
3. Sailboat
4. Mad/Sad/Glad
5. Timeline
6. Starfish

Then repeat, adjusting based on Sprint context.
