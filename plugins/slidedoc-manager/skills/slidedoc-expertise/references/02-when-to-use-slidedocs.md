# When to Use Slidedocs vs. Presentations vs. Documents

> **Source:** Duarte Inc. resources, Nancy Duarte's ebook and HBR article.

## Decision Framework

### Use a Slidedoc When...

| Scenario | Why Slidedoc Works |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Pre-read material** before a meeting | Gives decision-makers time to process dense info before the conversation |
| **Leave-behind** after a presentation | Provides the detail that cinematic slides cannot carry |
| **No presenter available** to walk through content | Visuals + text are self-explanatory |
| **In-depth research, analysis, or strategy** | Audience needs time to consider and process |
| **Decision-making material** | Dense, detailed information with visual clarity |
| **Knowledge sharing across teams** | Asynchronous consumption at reader's pace |
| **Feedback collection** | People can review carefully and respond thoughtfully |

### Use Projected Presentation Slides When...

- You are **delivering content live** to an audience
- You need **visual-first, minimal-text** designs (cinematic slides)
- A presenter is **physically present** to narrate and explain
- The goal is **persuasion and emotional engagement** in the moment
- The "Glance Test" applies: audience must grasp each slide in ~3 seconds

### Use a Traditional Document When...

- The content is **purely textual** with no visual enhancement needed
- The material is **formal/legal** (contracts, policies, specifications)
- The audience expects a **linear, long-form reading** experience
- **Version control and tracked changes** are essential

## The "Both" Problem

When someone says "we need slides for both projecting and leaving behind,"
Duarte's answer is clear: **you need two different decks**.

> "You wouldn't use your toothbrush to comb your hair just because it has bristles
> like your hairbrush."

### The Conversion Strategy

1. Create the **cinematic presentation** first (visual-first, minimal text)
2. Convert to a Slidedoc using **Notes View** in presentation software
3. Three formatting options for the leave-behind:
   - **Default template**: Add context below the slide image (appendix style)
   - **Adjusted template**: Cinematic visual + callout visual + supplemental text
   - **Landscape layout**: Varied typesetting to call attention to different elements

## Common Slidedoc Use Cases in Organizations

- Executive briefings and board decks
- Quarterly business reviews
- Project proposals and business cases
- Training and onboarding materials
- Research reports and competitive analyses
- Product roadmaps and strategy documents
- Internal newsletters and team updates
- Sales enablement materials
